## ⚡ Quick Summary
Threat actors have been identified using a new social engineering technique called InstallFix, which tricks users into executing malicious commands under the guise of installing legitimate command line interface (CLI) tools. This technique is a variation of the ClickFix method and involves fake install guides for Claude Code, aiming to push infostealers onto victim machines. The primary goal of these attacks is to steal sensitive information from the compromised systems.

## 🛠 Technical Analysis
The InstallFix technique involves distributing fake installation guides for CLI tools, specifically targeting users who are looking to install Claude Code. These guides are designed to appear legitimate, providing step-by-step instructions that include running malicious commands. Once executed, these commands download and install infostealers, which are then used to exfiltrate sensitive data from the compromised systems. The use of social engineering tactics, such as fake install guides, highlights the importance of verifying the authenticity of installation instructions and being cautious when executing commands from untrusted sources.

## 🛡 Mitigation & Impact
To mitigate the risk of falling victim to InstallFix attacks, users should be extremely cautious when following installation guides from the internet. It is essential to verify the authenticity of the source and ensure that any commands executed are legitimate and necessary for the installation process. Organizations and individuals should also implement robust security measures, including up-to-date antivirus software, regular system updates, and employee education on social engineering tactics. The impact of these attacks can be significant, as infostealers can lead to the unauthorized access and theft of sensitive information, potentially resulting in financial loss, reputational damage, and legal consequences. By being aware of these threats and taking proactive measures, users can significantly reduce the risk of compromise and protect their sensitive data.
