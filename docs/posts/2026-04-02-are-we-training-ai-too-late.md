## ⚡ Quick Summary
The cybersecurity landscape is continually evolving, with new and sophisticated threats emerging every day. A recent article highlights the importance of expanding our field of view to include new, unique threat sources rather than relying solely on past, proven threat actors. This requires a proactive approach to cybersecurity, leveraging AI and machine learning to stay ahead of potential threats. The question remains, are we training our AI systems too late, allowing threats to slip through the cracks before we can respond effectively?

## 🛠 Technical Analysis
As cybersecurity experts, we understand the critical role AI plays in detecting and mitigating threats. However, if we are only training our AI systems on historical data and known threats, we risk being unprepared for novel attacks. This is where the concept of "expanded field of view" comes into play. By incorporating a broader range of data sources, including emerging threat intelligence, we can enhance our AI's ability to identify and respond to new threats in real-time. This might involve integrating feeds from various sources, such as dark web monitoring, social media analysis, and IoT device surveillance, to create a more comprehensive threat landscape. Moreover, implementing techniques like anomaly detection and predictive analytics can help identify potential threats before they materialize.

## 🛡 Mitigation & Impact
The implications of training AI too late can be severe, leading to undetected breaches, data compromised, and reputational damage. To mitigate this risk, cybersecurity teams must prioritize the development of more agile and adaptive AI systems. This can be achieved by investing in continuous training and updating of AI models, ensuring they remain effective against evolving threats. Furthermore, fostering a culture of collaboration between human analysts and AI systems is crucial, allowing for the rapid dissemination of new threat intelligence and facilitating more effective incident response. By taking a proactive and holistic approach to AI training, we can significantly enhance our cybersecurity posture, reducing the likelihood of successful attacks and protecting sensitive assets from emerging threats.
