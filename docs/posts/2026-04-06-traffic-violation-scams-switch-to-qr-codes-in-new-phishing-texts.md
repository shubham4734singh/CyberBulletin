## ⚡ Quick Summary
A new wave of traffic violation scams has emerged, leveraging QR codes in phishing text messages to trick recipients into divulging personal and financial information. Impersonating state courts across the United States, scammers send fake "Notice of Default" messages, pressuring targets to scan a QR code that directs them to a phishing site. This site demands a payment of $6.99 while surreptitiously stealing sensitive information. The use of QR codes in these scams represents an evolution in tactics, aiming to exploit the growing trust and usage of QR codes for various transactions and information sharing.

## 🛠 Technical Analysis
From a technical standpoint, the incorporation of QR codes into phishing scams signifies a clever attempt to bypass traditional phishing detection mechanisms. QR codes can be easily generated and distributed, making them an attractive tool for scammers. The phishing sites associated with these QR codes are likely designed to be highly convincing, potentially including official-looking templates and language to manipulate victims into submitting their information. The request for a $6.99 payment serves as a pretext to collect financial details, which can then be used for fraudulent activities. Moreover, the sites may employ various tactics to evade detection, such as using HTTPS to appear legitimate and frequently changing domains to avoid blacklisting. As a cybersecurity expert, it's clear that these scams rely on a combination of social engineering and technical deception to succeed.

## 🛡 Mitigation & Impact
To mitigate the impact of these scams, it's essential for individuals to exercise extreme caution when receiving unsolicited texts, especially those that create a sense of urgency or demand immediate payment. Never scan QR codes from unknown sources, as they can lead to malicious sites designed to steal data. Furthermore, verifying the authenticity of any message purporting to be from a court or legal authority by contacting the relevant institution directly is crucial. On a broader scale, these scams highlight the need for ongoing public awareness campaigns about the risks associated with QR code phishing and the importance of vigilance in the digital age. Organizations and individuals must remain vigilant and continually update their security practices to combat evolving phishing tactics. By understanding the technical and social engineering aspects of these scams, we can better protect against them and reduce their effectiveness.
