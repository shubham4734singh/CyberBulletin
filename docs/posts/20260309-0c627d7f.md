## ⚡ Quick Summary
Microsoft Teams is introducing a new feature to enhance meeting security by automatically tagging third-party bots that attempt to join meetings. This feature will allow meeting organizers to control whether these bots can join, providing an additional layer of security and privacy.

## 🛠 Technical Analysis
The implementation of this feature is a response to potential security threats posed by unauthorized bots joining Microsoft Teams meetings. By tagging third-party bots in meeting lobbies, Microsoft aims to prevent malicious activities such as data breaches, eavesdropping, or the dissemination of malware. This move underlines the importance of vigilance in collaborative platforms, where the presence of unknown or unverified entities can pose significant risks.

## 🛡 Mitigation & Impact
The impact of this feature will be significant for organizations relying on Microsoft Teams for communication and collaboration. By giving meeting organizers the power to vet and approve bots before they can join a meeting, Microsoft is providing a crucial tool for mitigating potential security threats. This feature is expected to enhance the overall security posture of Microsoft Teams, making it a more trustworthy platform for sensitive discussions and collaborations. As a result, users can expect a safer and more controlled environment for their online meetings, reducing the risk of unauthorized access or malicious activities.
