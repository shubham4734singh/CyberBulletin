## ⚡ Quick Summary
Microsoft has announced plans to deprecate legacy TLS (Transport Layer Security) connections for POP and IMAP email clients in Exchange Online, starting in July 2026. This move aims to enhance the security posture of Exchange Online by phasing out outdated and vulnerable encryption protocols. The deprecation will affect email clients that rely on legacy TLS versions to connect to Exchange Online, potentially disrupting email services for users who have not updated their clients to support modern TLS versions.

## 🛠 Technical Analysis
From a technical standpoint, the deprecation of legacy TLS connections is a necessary step to protect against potential security threats. Legacy TLS versions, such as TLS 1.0 and TLS 1.1, have known vulnerabilities that can be exploited by attackers to intercept or manipulate sensitive data. By moving to modern TLS versions, such as TLS 1.2 and TLS 1.3, Microsoft can significantly reduce the risk of security breaches and ensure a more secure connection for email clients. Additionally, this move aligns with industry best practices and recommendations from organizations such as the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) and the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).

## 🛡 Mitigation & Impact
To mitigate the impact of this change, organizations and users should take immediate action to update their email clients to support modern TLS versions. This may involve updating software, configuring email clients to use modern TLS, or migrating to more secure email protocols such as MAPI or HTTP. The impact of this change will be significant for users who rely on legacy email clients or have not updated their systems to support modern TLS versions. Disruptions to email services can be expected, and users may experience errors or unable to connect to Exchange Online using outdated clients. It is essential for organizations to assess their email infrastructure and take necessary steps to ensure a smooth transition to modern TLS versions, minimizing the risk of security breaches and ensuring uninterrupted email services.
