## ⚡ Quick Summary
The traditional reliance on identity checks alone is no longer sufficient in today's complex cybersecurity landscape. Attackers have become adept at exploiting stolen session tokens and compromised devices, highlighting the need for a more comprehensive security approach. As outlined by Specops Software, Zero Trust strategies are increasingly recognizing the importance of continuous device verification, alongside identity checks, to fortify defenses against sophisticated threats.

## 🛠 Technical Analysis
From a technical standpoint, the limitations of identity checks become apparent when considering the ease with which attackers can obtain and utilize stolen credentials or session tokens. Once an attacker gains access to a network or system through a compromised identity, they can often move laterally without being detected by traditional identity-based security measures. This is where device security comes into play. By continuously verifying the security posture of devices, organizations can identify and mitigate potential threats before they escalate. This includes monitoring for signs of compromise, ensuring devices are updated with the latest security patches, and enforcing strict access controls based on the device's trustworthiness. The integration of device security with Zero Trust models enhances the overall security posture by adding an additional layer of verification, making it more difficult for attackers to exploit vulnerabilities.

## 🛡 Mitigation & Impact
To effectively mitigate the risks associated with compromised devices and stolen session tokens, organizations should adopt a holistic security strategy that combines robust identity checks with continuous device verification. This approach not only strengthens the overall security posture but also supports compliance with increasingly stringent data protection regulations. The impact of neglecting device security can be severe, ranging from data breaches and financial loss to reputational damage. By recognizing the shared responsibility between identity and device security, organizations can proactively protect their assets and maintain the trust of their customers and stakeholders. Implementing a Zero Trust framework that includes device security measures is a critical step towards achieving this balanced security approach, ensuring that organizations are well-equipped to face the evolving threats of the digital landscape.
